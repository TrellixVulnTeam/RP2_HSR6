About Us!

Through the COVID-19 pandemic and the shift in modern technology, much of our communication platforms are online forums. As such, there is an expectation to be able to communicate via online platforms. For many neurodivergent individuals, methods of communication are different from their neurotypical peers. Krishnapriya Nair and the Comm-unity team aim to create a social media-style web application for middle school-aged children on the autism spectrum to practice their communication skills and create a sense of community among their peers. Typically, this process is done through Social Skills Training Programs (SSTP) creating a right and wrong way of communicating. Rather than introducing a standard of correctness for communication, the aim of this project is to provide an outlet for individuals on the spectrum to practice and improve their social skills in an environment we hope is most comfortable.


The Team!

Krishnapriya Nair is an undergraduate Computer Science student at UC Davis. She has volunteered with organization such as WeEmbrace working with neurodivergent children and adults teaching dance classes. Through Comm-unity, Krishna aims to use her skill set in Computer Science in the Autism community.

Kurt Eiselt is an Associate Professor of Teaching & Vice-Chair at UC Davis. For the past fifteen years, Eiselt's focus has been on improving the introductory computing experience for students and creating outreach programs to broaden participation in computing. He is especially interested in improving access to computing education for students with disabilities. Most recently, Eiselt has been working on the development of workshops combining computer programming educations with social skills training for teens and pre-teens on the autism spectrum.
